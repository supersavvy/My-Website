My Passions!

Girls Who Code was life-changing for me. I learned the basics of Python, C++, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and other important languages. I had the opportunity to attend important tech events like the CybHer Security Conference at Stanford University, and explore prominent technical companies, including: Juniper Networks, GoodReads, Twitch, and multiple branches of Amazon like Amazon Web Services and Lab126.

Undeniable Life is an online magazine targeted towards young teenage girls. Most of the content concerns topics like relationships and lifestyles, but I applied for an internship to write for and manage the science, technology, and current event columns. I write frequently for all three columns, and am also the head for the weekly "Breaking News" segment, one of our main features.

Gifts of Awesome

Graphic and Web Design Inc. is a non-profit that helps both non-profits and high school students in conjunction. GAWD Inc. meets with non-profits that need to expand their digital platform, and gives these digital projects to students in an effort to help the community develop programming and web design skills. As the Director of Publicity, I reached out to these non-profits and other technical experts, and connected them to students. It was an extremely fulfilling experience that connected me to a lot of professional and student programmers.

I've done Speech and Debate for my entire life, and became a varsity debate member in freshman year. It's taught me so much, from how to create a sturdy argument backed up with evidence, to being a confident public speaker. I've been to many tournaments, from UC Berkeley to Stanford University. I've won top speaker and advanced to late elimination rounds at many tournaments as well, including Santa Clara University and the JV Western Championships.

I also try to give back to my team as much as possible. I dedicate afterschool on Monday every week to judging practice rounds and giving topic lectures to our novice debaters, so that our team can continue to be the best around.

Stop Hunger Now is a non-profit that aims to package food as inexpensively as possible to send to third-world countries. As the past Secretary, current Vice President, and club Co-Founder of my school's Stop Hunger Now branch, I coordinate events with the organization so that students at our school have the opportunity to make an impact on the world around them through meal-packaging, soup kitchen volunteering, and informational presentations at elementary schools.

DECA is a finance and competition based program that allows students to simulate working in the economic world. By working with local businesses to analyze economic trends in my community, I placed top 10 at both regionals and states in freshman year, and 3rd place at regionals and top 8 at states in sophomore year. I learned a great deal about timme management and community outreach, and made a lot of amazing friends along the way.

Through Relay for Life, I've had the opportunity to learn more about how cancer affects the people around me, and I've had the pleasure of raising over $4,000 for research with my team.
